Hello There!

My name is Katrina Ezis and today I'd like to show you some of my work! But first, let me tell you a little bit about me. I'm a senior at the University of Washington, which means I'm about to be forced out into the real world real soon! I study informatics, but also like to camp, read, create, and learn. Now that we're done with introductions, let's get onto the good stuff!

Let's get started

Pocket Hipster This was one of my first websites ever build. It was created in just under 24 hours at Dubhacks, a Washington hackaton. It takes in an image and writes a free-verse poem for your enjoyment. It's like having a hipster with you wherever you go! We ended up winning second place for this, which was amazing and still one of my proudest moments to date!

Pinpoint is the product of design thinking and user research. It is an app that helps college students (or anyone, really) find events to go to nearby. This was one of the first projects that I did user interviews and created use cases! I also did a full write-up for it explaining our thinking and how we made our design choices.

Rate-A-Hipster is a CRUD application that used parse (may it rest in peace) to write little reviews about a couple of hipsters. I tried to use my best hipster design practices in making this website both functional, but also have a great user interface that is as hipster as can be!
